Activites

There were many adventures I explored, but here are two that I enjoyed most:

The Saint Vincent and the Grenadines Botanic Gardens is located in Kingstown, Saint Vincent and the Grenadines. Established in 1765, it is the oldest botanic garden in the Western Hemisphere. It is one of the most visited sites in Saint Vincent and the Grenadines.

Dark View Falls are two spectacular waterfalls, one above the other, cascading down high cliff faces and plunging into natural pools. The waterfalls flow all year from a tributary of the Richmond River. A natural Bamboo bridge spans the tumbling river, luring you to the two majestic falls.

Food & Community

Fresh seafood from the sea is a major part of the local cuisine. Cassava, spices, and many other ingredients. Besides Roasted Breadfruit & Fried Jackfish, which is the national dish, they have flavours like madongo dumplings made from native arrowroot, buljol salad made from salt fish or cod, and callaloo soup consisting of local leaf vegetables.

People in St. Vincent and the Grenadines are friendly, laid-back, and welcoming, with a strong sense of community, deeply rooted in their African heritage, and heavily influenced by British culture due to their colonial past, reflected in their language (English) and relaxed lifestyle; they are also known for their love of music, food, and outdoor activities.

Island Trip

Bequia is a small island, measuring 7 square miles with a population of approximately 5,300. It is part of the country of Saint Vincent and the Grenadines. Bequia means 'island of the clouds' in the language of the ancient Arawak. The island's name was also 'Becouya' as part of the Grenadines. Fun fact: Princess Margaret, who had a home on nearby Mustique, visited Bequia in the 1950s and had a beach renamed in her honour.
